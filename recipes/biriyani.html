Biriyani

Description

Biryani is a mixed rice dish that originated in South Asia. It is traditionally prepared using rice, meat (such as chicken, goat, or beef) or seafood, along with vegetables and aromatic spices. The dish was developed during the Mughal era in India, though its exact origin is still debated. Biryani is believed to have evolved from the Persian pilau rice dish.

Ingredients

  • Basmati Rice (long-grain)
  • Protein: Chicken, Mutton, or Vegetables
  • Ghee and/or Oil
  • Yogurt (Curd)
  • Onions (sliced and fried until brown)
  • Ginger-Garlic Paste
  • Green Chilies
  • Mint
  • Fresh Coriander (Cilantro)
  • Lemon Juice
  • Whole Spices (Whole Garam Masala)
  • Bay Leaves
  • Cinnamon Sticks
  • Cardamom (Green and Black)
  • Cloves
  • Star Anise
  • Shahi Jeera (Caraway seeds)
  • Biryani Masala Powder
  • Turmeric Powder
  • Red Chili Powder
  • Salt
  • Saffron (soaked in milk)
  • Kewra Water or Rose Water
  • Fried Onions (for garnish)

Steps

  1. Marinate the Protein: In a bowl, combine your choice of protein (chicken, mutton, or vegetables) with yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, red chili powder, turmeric, biryani masala, lemon juice, and salt. Let it marinate for at least 1 hour (overnight is best for deeper flavor).
  2. Fry the Onions: Slice onions thinly and deep-fry them in oil/ghee until dark golden brown (Birista). Set aside.
  3. Cook the Rice: Boil a large pot of water with whole spices (cinnamon, cardamom, cloves, bay leaf). Add soaked basmati rice and cook until it is only 70% done (still has a bite). Drain and set aside.
  4. Cook the Masala: In a heavy-bottomed pot, heat ghee. Add the marinated meat/veg and cook until the protein is sealed and the oil starts to separate from the gravy.
  5. Layering: Lower the heat. On top of the cooked masala, spread a layer of the par-boiled rice.
  6. Add Aromatics: Sprinkle fried onions, chopped mint, and coriander over the rice layer.
  7. Final Topping: Drizzle saffron milk and a little ghee over the very top.
  8. Dum (Steam): Seal the pot tightly with a lid (or foil) to trap the steam. Cook on very low heat for 15–20 minutes (25–30 if using mutton).
  9. Fluff: Gently mix the layers with a fork before serving, being careful not to break the long rice grains.
