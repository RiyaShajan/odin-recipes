Kerala Fish Curry (Meen Curry)

Description

Kerala Fish Curry, also known as Meen Curry, is a traditional dish from Kerala cuisine. It is prepared using fresh fish cooked in a tangy and spicy gravy made with kokum or tamarind, red chili powder, coriander, and coconut oil. This curry is commonly served with rice and is known for its bold flavors and aroma.

Ingredients

  • Fish (seer fish, kingfish, or sardines)
  • Coconut Oil
  • Shallots (sliced)
  • Ginger-Garlic Paste
  • Curry Leaves
  • Green Chilies
  • Kokum (Kudampuli) or Tamarind
  • Turmeric Powder
  • Red Chili Powder
  • Coriander Powder
  • Fenugreek Seeds
  • Salt
  • Water

Steps

  1. Prepare the Tamarind: Soak kudampuli or tamarind in warm water and set aside.
  2. Sauté Aromatics: Heat coconut oil in a clay pot or pan. Add fenugreek seeds and let them splutter. Add shallots, ginger-garlic paste, green chilies, and curry leaves. Sauté until soft.
  3. Add Spices: Lower the heat and add turmeric, red chili powder, and coriander powder. Mix well to avoid burning.
  4. Make the Gravy: Add water and the soaked tamarind along with its water. Bring to a gentle boil.
  5. Add Fish: Carefully add the fish pieces into the simmering curry.
  6. Cook: Cover and cook on low heat until the fish is cooked and the gravy thickens.
  7. Final Touch: Drizzle a little coconut oil and add fresh curry leaves. Switch off the heat.
  8. Rest and Serve: Let the curry rest for 15–20 minutes before serving. Serve hot with rice.
