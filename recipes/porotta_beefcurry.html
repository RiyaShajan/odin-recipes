Porotta & Beef Curry

Description

Porotta and Beef Curry is a popular South Indian dish, especially famous in Kerala cuisine. Porotta is a flaky, layered flatbread made from refined flour, while beef curry is a rich and spicy preparation cooked with aromatic spices, coconut slices, and slow-cooked beef. Together, they form a classic comfort meal loved across the region.

Ingredients

  • Beef (cut into medium pieces)
  • Onions (thinly sliced)
  • Ginger-Garlic Paste
  • Green Chilies
  • Curry Leaves
  • Coconut Slices
  • Coconut Oil
  • Turmeric Powder
  • Red Chili Powder
  • Coriander Powder
  • Black Pepper Powder
  • Garam Masala
  • Salt
  • Water
  • Porotta (for serving)

Steps

  1. Marinate the Beef: Clean the beef and marinate it with turmeric powder, chili powder, pepper powder, salt, and ginger-garlic paste. Rest for at least 30 minutes.
  2. Pressure Cook: Add the marinated beef to a pressure cooker with water and cook until tender. Set aside.
  3. Prepare the Masala: Heat coconut oil in a pan. Add coconut slices and fry until golden brown. Remove and keep aside.
  4. Sauté Onions: In the same oil, add onions, green chilies, curry leaves, and sauté until onions turn dark brown.
  5. Add Spices: Add coriander powder, chili powder, garam masala, and mix well on low heat.
  6. Combine Beef: Add cooked beef along with its stock. Mix well and simmer until the gravy thickens.
  7. Final Touch: Add fried coconut slices and adjust salt. Cook for a few more minutes.
  8. Serve Hot: Serve the beef curry hot with soft, flaky porottas.
